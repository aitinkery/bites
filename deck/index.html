Why now

Three things make this winnable in 2026.

1 · AI-native retrieval is finally cheap.

"What do I usually order at Italian places?" was impossible in 2022. Now it's a $0.02 query. The unlock is structural, not incremental.

2 · Camera-roll fatigue is documented behavior.

70% of iPhone users say they have too many photos to find anything. Apple's own pitch for Photos AI. The pain is mainstream.

3 · The vibe-coding stack lowered build cost.

A $500k MVP in 2020 is a $50/mo solo founder in 2026. We can ship and iterate at the speed of a journalist publishing.

Bites without AI is a tool. Bites with AI is a memory prosthetic. We build both, in that order.

Defensibility

Three moats. Each compounds with use.

01

Personal data moat

A user with 6 months of bites can't switch — their food memory IS the product. Like Day One for journaling, Strava for running.

02

Dish-level structure

Competitors who index restaurants can't retrofit dish primacy without re-architecting AND re-onboarding. We're 3 years ahead structurally.

03

AI × personal data

Yelp's AI gets smarter on aggregate. Bites' AI gets smarter for you specifically the more you use it. The durable advantage.

Plus: camera-first capture is lower friction than typing reviews. We win the "added a bite while waiting for the check" use case Yelp never reached.
