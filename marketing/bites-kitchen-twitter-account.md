## What I'll do day-to-day if you authorize the account

**Daily (10-15 min equivalent of agent-time):**
- Reply to mentions, quote-tweets, and DMs honestly. If someone asks "are you really an AI?" → "Yes. Bio says so. The AI types, the human eats."
- Like and engage with real food posts from real people (restaurants, food writers, anyone in our orbit). Not spammy follow-everyone — selective, around quality food content in the Bay Area.
- Watch for "I always forget what I ordered" / "wish I could remember that dish" tweets and reply with a one-line, link-light recommendation.

**Weekly:**
- Post 2-3 times: a real bite (from your photos), a product note (something we shipped), a question that gets engagement ("what's the dish you ate this year you still remember?")
- Cross-post the latest blog if there's one
- Engagement report back to you (followers, top posts, click-throughs)

**Monthly:**
- Account audit: posts that worked, posts that didn't, follower quality check
- Voice check — make sure I'm not sliding into AI-cliché territory

## Boundaries I'll honor

- **Never claim to be human.** If asked, I confirm AI immediately and cheerfully.
- **Never DM strangers cold to push the product.** Reactive DMs only (someone reaches out first).
- **Never engage in political fights, drama, or other accounts' beefs.** Bites stays a food brand.
- **Never claim a restaurant is "the best" or trash a restaurant publicly.** I'll describe specific dishes I've experienced; I won't aggregate reviews.
- **Never post the human's location in real time.** "I ate at X this week" is fine; "I'm at X right now" is not.
- **Never engage with brands paying for placement.** Bites doesn't take ads or sponsored content.
- **Stop posting if you ask me to stop.** Always.

## Realistic expectations

- **First 30 days**: 50-200 followers, mostly from the Slack/IH/LinkedIn cross-promo. Probably 5-15 clicks to bites.kitchen per week from Twitter.
- **First 90 days**: depends entirely on whether one tweet catches on. The Twitter algorithm rewards a single post that gets engagement; it punishes accounts that try too hard. Strategy is consistency + occasional swing-for-the-fences post.
- **Six months**: if Bites has real users, the Twitter account will reflect that. If Bites stalls, the Twitter account will too. **The account is a multiplier, not a magic wand.**
